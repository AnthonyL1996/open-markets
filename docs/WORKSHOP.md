# Steam Workshop description

> Source for the Steam Workshop page. Steam uses BBCode, not Markdown — paste the block below directly
> into the Workshop item description. Keep the "Coming soon" framing honest: anything not yet shipped
> stays under Roadmap so subscribers aren't misled.

```bbcode
[h1]Open Markets — a living trade economy[/h1]
Vanilla Cities: Skylines treats the outside world as a faceless void — trucks haul goods in and out and the money is just abstract tax. [b]Open Markets turns that void into a REAL commodity market[/b], with prices that move, partners that matter, and trades that have consequences.

[b]Ambient and opt-in.[/b] No building to plop, no tech to unlock. Enable it and your existing city's trade comes alive. Fully save-safe and removal-safe — uninstall any time and your city still loads.

[hr][/hr]
[h1]What you get now[/h1]

[h2]📈 A dynamic commodity market[/h2]
[list]
[*]Every traded resource — oil, grain, ore, goods and more — has a daily price that drifts, mean-reverts, and gets hit by random [b]market events[/b] (shocks of ±25–50% lasting several days).
[*][b]Bid/ask spread:[/b] you sell exports a little below the market and buy imports a little above it, just like a real exchange. Profit comes from value-add chains and good timing.
[*][b]Elasticity:[/b] dump 50,000 tons of goods and you depress your own selling price. Where and when you trade actually matters.
[/list]

[h2]🏙️ Named partner cities[/h2]
[list]
[*]Your anonymous outside connections become named partner cities, each with its own per-commodity prices and your lifetime trade balance.
[/list]

[h2]📊 The Markets dashboard[/h2]
[list]
[*]A draggable panel: commodities × partners, live price index with green/red movement, [b]price-history sparklines[/b], and [b]·BUY / ·SELL[/b] speculation markers.
[*][b]Click any cell to embargo[/b] that trade route — cut off a partner for a commodity at will.
[/list]

[h2]💰 Balance-of-trade report[/h2]
[list]
[*]A standalone panel listing each partner's lifetime exported / imported / net §, sorted best-first, with totals.
[/list]

[h2]🔔 Speculation & event alerts[/h2]
[list]
[*]One-shot Chirper alerts when a commodity becomes a notable [b]buy (cheap)[/b] or [b]sell (dear)[/b], and when a market event starts. The board flags the opportunity; you decide whether to act.
[/list]

[h2]🚚 Optional warehouse automation (Industries DLC)[/h2]
[list]
[*]Opt in and the mod steers your Industries warehouses by price — fill when cheap, empty when dear — while respecting anything you've set manually.
[/list]

[h2]🎚️ Tunable[/h2]
[list]
[*]Live sliders for price volatility, elasticity strength, traffic-driver deadband, and the bid/ask spread. Don't like a mechanic? Turn it down to zero.
[/list]

[hr][/hr]
[h1]🔜 Coming soon — Online: "Play With Friends"[/h1]
[i]In active development. The single-player economy above is complete and playable today; everything in this section is the roadmap. It will always be an optional layer that degrades gracefully offline.[/i]

[list]
[*][b]A shared market[/b] — link your economy to your friends'. Your neighbour dumping 5,000 oil actually moves [i]your[/i] price. Real cross-city cause and effect.
[*][b]A resting order book[/b] — post standing buy/sell offers between cities; trade a friend who's offline and wake up to filled orders.
[*][b]Loans & financial instruments[/b] — lend cash to a friend at your own interest rate, write futures contracts, insure each other against market shocks, issue city bonds, and build a credit rating from your track record.
[*][b]Cartels & diplomacy[/b] — form supply pacts to pump prices (and feel the temptation to defect), sign free-trade agreements, or wage tariff wars on a rival.
[*][b]An authored crisis engine[/b] — world-shaping events ripple through everyone's economy at once: blockaded trade routes, failed harvests, labour strikes, tech booms. Shared shocks that turn a quiet week into a scramble.
[*][b]Friendly competition[/b] — friend leaderboards with a rotating weekly metric, weekly head-to-head matchups, and seasons. Scored fairly by rate, not hours played.
[*][b]City profiles & social texture[/b] — see your trade partners' name, age, population, happiness and specialty; leave a pinned message; needle a friend with a chirp.
[/list]

[hr][/hr]
[h1]Safe to try[/h1]
[list]
[*][b]Save-safe & removal-safe.[/b] Open Markets stores data in its own save section and never writes anything the base game can't read back. Uninstall and your city still loads cleanly.
[*][b]Plays nice.[/b] Designed to coexist with transfer/cargo mods like TM:PE and MoreEffectiveTransfer. Hit a conflict? Please report it.
[*][b]Online is opt-in[/b] — with it off there is zero network activity.
[/list]

[h1]Requirements[/h1]
[list]
[*]Cities: Skylines 1 (this is for CS1, [b]not[/b] Cities: Skylines II).
[*]The [b]Harmony[/b] mod (you'll be auto-subscribed).
[*]Industries DLC is optional — only the warehouse-automation feature uses it; everything else works with no DLC.
[/list]

Source, issues, and the full design roadmap on GitHub.
```
